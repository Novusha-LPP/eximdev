## 1. Why This Guide Exists

This guide explains **what an Open Point is**, **what it is not**, and **how to use the Open Points Module** in a simple way. It is meant for everyone, even those who are not from a technical background.

---

## 2. What Is an Open Point?

An **Open Point** is an important action that must be completed and tracked.

An item is an Open Point **only if all three are clear**:

* **Who** will do it (Responsibility)
* **What** exactly needs to be done (Discussion Point)
* **By when** it must be completed (Target Date)

If any of these are missing, it should **not** be an Open Point.

**Example:**

* "Finalize transport approval process by 20th Feb â€“ Owner: Ramesh"

---

## 3. What Is NOT an Open Point?

Do **not** create Open Points for the following:

* General discussions or meetings without a decision
* Ideas or suggestions that are not finalized
* Daily routine work (regular job duties)
* Information-only messages (FYI, announcements)
* Personal reminders

These do not need tracking and should stay outside the Open Points Module.

---

## 4. Open Point vs Normal Task

| Normal Task            | Open Point                            |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Personal or small work | Important work affecting team/project |
| Short-term             | Tracked until fully closed            |
| Not reviewed regularly | Reviewed by team & management         |

Only important items needing follow-up and visibility should be Open Points.

## 6. Ownership Rules (Very Important)

* Every Open Point must have **one owner only** (Responsibility).
* Other people may help, but **one person is responsible**.
* The owner must:
  * Give updates by changing the **Status** (Red, Yellow, Green).
  * Complete work on time.
  * Ask the Project Owner if the date needs to be changed.

No Open Point should exist without a clear owner.

---

## 7. What Is the Open Points Module?

The **Open Points Module** is a single place where all important pending actions are recorded.

It ensures:

* Everyone sees the same information.
* No action is forgotten.
* Clear accountability via a **dashboard** that shows progress charts.

Think of it as a **live action register for the entire organization**.

## 9. Dashboard & Tracking

The home screen provides a quick view of everything:

* **My Tasks Overview**: A pie chart showing your personal pending vs. completed items.
* **Projects Health**: A summary of how all projects are doing.
